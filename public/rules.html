RULES & SAFETY

Read this before you roll up. These guidelines keep the vibes high, the grounds safe, and everyone around you able to enjoy AMPED UP 26.

What You Can Bring

ENTRY CHECKLIST
  • Clear bags up to 12" x 6" x 12"
  • Small clutches/fanny packs up to 6.5" x 4.5" (do not need to be clear)
  • Hydration packs (must be empty upon entry, 2L max)
  • Empty reusable water bottles (plastic or metal)
  • Non-aerosol sunscreen (travel size, 3.4 oz max)
  • Hats, sunglasses, bandanas
  • Blankets or beach towels
  • Earplugs
  • Portable phone chargers/power banks
  • Disposable cameras or small point-and-shoot cameras (no detachable lenses)
  • Sealed snacks (e.g., granola bars, trail mix)
  • Prescription medications (must be in original container with matching ID)
  • Service animals (with proper documentation)

Items Not Allowed

Security will enforce these rules. Violation may result in denied entry or removal without refund.

  • NO bags larger than 12" x 6" x 12" or non-clear bags (except small clutches/fanny packs up to 6.5" x 4.5")
  • NO outside food and beverages (including alcohol, energy drinks, cans, or glass bottles)
  • NO glass containers of any kind
  • NO weapons or weapon-like items (guns, knives, scissors, pepper spray, tasers, brass knuckles)
  • NO illegal substances or drug paraphernalia
  • NO aerosol products (spray sunscreen, bug spray, perfumes)
  • NO professional audio/video/photography equipment (detachable lenses, tripods, monopods, drones, GoPros if restricted)
  • NO laser pointers, selfie sticks, totems over height limits, flagpoles, or tripods
  • NO fireworks, explosives, sparklers, or flammable liquids
  • NO pets or emotional support animals (service animals only permitted)
  • NO large sports gear (skateboards, scooters, bikes, hoverboards, balls, frisbees)
  • NO musical instruments (drums, horns, etc.)
  • NO large inflatables, furniture, hammocks, or air mattresses
  • NO noise-making devices (air horns, megaphones, portable speakers)
  • NO chains, spiked jewelry, studded belts, or other items that can be used as weapons
  • NO walkie-talkies, two-way radios, or signal jammers
  • NO unauthorized vending items (merch, food, flyers, promotional materials)
  • NO water guns, balloons, or spray bottles
  • NO masks or full-face coverings (unless for health/safety)
  • NO unmanned aerial vehicles (drones, remote-controlled devices)

Health & Safety Acknowledgement

By entering the festival grounds, you acknowledge and agree to follow all health and safety rules set forth by the organizers. This includes complying with security screenings, respecting personal space, staying hydrated, and seeking medical assistance if needed.

You understand that participation in large events carries inherent risks, and you voluntarily assume responsibility for your own health and safety while attending. Festival staff, medical teams, and security are available to support a safe environment, and failure to follow guidelines may result in removal without refund.

On-Site Medical Services

Your health and well-being are our highest priority. Professional medical staff, including licensed EMTs and nurses, are stationed on-site and equipped to respond to both minor and serious health concerns.

First Aid Tents are clearly marked and located near main entrances, central pathways, and major stage areas for easy access. These tents are stocked with basic supplies for common issues like dehydration, heat exhaustion, cuts, or sprains, and are also prepared to handle emergency situations until additional medical services arrive if necessary.

In addition to staffed tents, roaming medical teams will be moving through festival grounds to assist attendees directly in the crowd. If you or someone near you requires help, you can alert any staff member, volunteer, or security personnel, who will quickly contact medical support. All medical services at the festival are free of charge, confidential, and available to everyone.

We strongly encourage attendees to stay hydrated, wear sun protection, and take breaks in shaded areas. Please don’t wait until an issue becomes severe—seek assistance right away if you feel unwell. Our team is here to ensure a safe and enjoyable experience for all.

Festival Code of Conduct

We are committed to creating a safe, inclusive, and enjoyable environment for all attendees, staff, and performers. By entering the festival grounds, you agree to follow these guidelines:

  • Respect others – Treat fellow attendees, staff, and performers with kindness and courtesy.
  • Zero tolerance for harassment – Harassment, discrimination, or abusive behavior of any kind will not be tolerated.
  • Keep it safe – Follow security instructions, stay aware of your surroundings, and look out for one another.
  • Celebrate responsibly – Enjoy the festival without endangering yourself or others. Excessive intoxication or unsafe behavior may result in removal.
  • Protect the space – Help keep the festival grounds clean. Dispose of waste properly and respect shared areas.
  • No unauthorized sales or promotions
